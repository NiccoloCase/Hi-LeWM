# hope2 Experiment 4: Generated-Subgoal Reachability

Source: [hi_diagnostics_report_2026-05-09.md](/gpfs/home2/scur0200/main/roadmap/hi_diagnostics_report_2026-05-09.md:542)

This note keeps only the `hope2` rows from the `Actual results` table.

## What is happening

This experiment asks whether the low-level planner can reach subgoals generated by the high-level planner, not real dataset subgoals.

`d25 hh1` is reachable but not especially clean: step 1 terminal error is `0.1639`, dataset distance is `5.0071`, and same-trajectory distance is `1.2698`. Switching to `d25 hh2` makes the generated subgoals much easier to hit in latent space: step 1 error drops to `0.0128` and step 2 is `0.0161`. Dataset distance also improves slightly (`4.9118`, `4.8708`). But the first intermediate subgoal is not more temporally faithful to the source trajectory, since `same_traj_distance` worsens from `1.2698` to `2.7779`; the second subgoal recovers to `0.9386`. So `hh2` gives more reachable subgoals, but the first waypoint can still be temporally misplaced.

`d50 hh1` is the clear failure case: step 1 terminal error jumps to `0.5289`, with dataset distance `5.6839` and same-trajectory distance `2.8625`. Splitting the horizon into `d50 hh2` dramatically improves latent reachability: step 1 error drops to `0.1086` and step 2 is `0.1173`. Dataset distance also improves to `5.5547` and `5.3101`. The same nuance remains: step 1 same-trajectory distance is still poor at `5.2670`, but step 2 becomes much better at `1.1151`.

## Takeaway

For `hope2`, one long generated macro subgoal is the worst regime, especially at `d50`. Splitting the plan into two generated subgoals makes the targets much easier for the low-level planner to realize, which supports the idea that `hh2` produces better subgoal geometry than `hh1`. The remaining weakness is temporal alignment: the first generated midpoint can still be off relative to the real trajectory even when it is easy to hit in latent space.
